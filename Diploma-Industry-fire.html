DIPLOMA | Industrial Safety & Fire Safety Engineering

DURATION : 3 YRS. (6 SEMESTERS)

The Department of Industrial and Fire Safety offers a Diploma in Industrial and Fire Safety designed to equip students with comprehensive knowledge and skills essential for ensuring workplace safety. The curriculum is tailored to industry standards, emphasizing practical expertise through hands-on projects and experiential learning. Students engage regularly with industry experts and respected organizations in occupational safety and fire prevention, gaining invaluable practical experience and insights crucial for their professional development in this critical field.

University is commencing Diploma in Engineering from 2020-21. The nomenclature of the degree are as follows:

  • Diploma in Fire safety and Industrial safety – 60 Seats

Vision

  • To advance, develop and promote coordinated injury prevention and product safety strategies, particularly in areas of new and emerging technologies and in areas that are underserved by other organizations.

Mission

  • To create world-class facilities for advance level of teaching and industrial training.
  • To continuously grow and become ountain head among institutes of Fire Engineering & Safety Management education in India.
  • To develop students as global citizens with conscience, commitment and dedication.
  • To have collaboration with leading industries & Companies of the world to provide opportunities to the students for best industrial training & seek placement avenues abroad.

PROGRAMME EDUCATIONAL OBJECTIVES (PEOS)

  • ✔ Possess a mastery of Health safety and environment knowledge and safety management skills, to reach higher levels in their profession.
  • ✔ Knowledgeable safety Engineer rendering professional expertise to the industrial and societal needs at national and global level subject to legal requirements.
  • ✔ Well communicate the information on Health safety and environment facilitating collaboration with experts across various disciplines so as to create and execute safe methodology in complex engineering activities.

PROGRAMME OUTCOME

  • Identify and select suitable chemicals (industrial, inflammable liquid) usable in the workplace following safety precautions.
  • Identify, select, and execute the application of different types of extinguishers, hoses, and hose fittings.
  • Select and prepare the hydrant and pump system for proper application.
  • Plan and execute the concept of hydraulics in the workplace.
  • Select and categorize electrical hazards and risks and their mitigation.
  • Identify and select methods of using ladders in the practical field.
  • Select the BA set and its application in appropriate places.
  • Identify and use small and special gears.
  • Plan and execute elementary treatment at any incidental spot.
  • Utilize knots and hitches in different special jobs and fires.
  • Plan and execute to uplift various gears with proper techniques, carry out Hazard and Risk evaluation selecting the proper method of rescue and F.F.
  • Analyze the concept of accident causation and prevention, accident investigation, analysis, and safety management.
  • Select and apply provisions related to safety, health, and welfare in respect to the Factory Act, 1948.
  • Assess available resources and their proper use.
  • Interpret appropriate techniques of CPR.
  • Identify the importance of lighting, ventilation, work-related stress, and its measurement.
  • Plan and execute fixed firefighting installations for their effective utilization.
  • Select and use PPE, demonstrate its care and maintenance.
  • Select Automatic Fire Detection cum Alarm System to plan their effective utilization.
  • Plan and execute fire station administration.
  • Identify communication systems in different organizations and their scope of use.
  • Get accustomed to different fire situations and firefighting using extinguishers.
  • Plan and execute disaster response practices, IRS/JRT, and salvage techniques.
  • Select and apply the correct rescue method.
  • Categorize building constructions that can ensure fire and life safety.
  • Plan and execute fire protection measures based on construction and occupancy.
  • Plan and survey Airport and Aircraft, port and ship for rescue systems and firefighting systems on them.
  • Identify occupational hazards associated with different dangerous chemicals, dust, gases, mist, vapors, etc., to plan and execute rescue operations in these cases.
  • Comply with safety precautions while working at heights, in confined spaces, and with the work permit system.
  • Identify the characteristics of various fire suppression agents, including water, and ensure safety in the manual and mechanical handling of materials.
  • Demonstrate hazard evaluation and risk analysis exercises.
